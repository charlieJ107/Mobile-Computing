I can still remember that once when I was a child, my parents went away together and needed me to solve my lunch by myself. My mother will tell me how to cook a bowl of delicious noodles, how to boil water, how to cook noodles, how to prepare noodles side dishes and so on. Although I have been able to feed myself by cooking in another country after many years, I still can't forget how my mother gave me simple and precise instructions to complete the cooking process according to her introduction. Even now, when cooking, I still need to refer to the recipe and divide "how to make a dish" into the most straightforward basic processes, such as cutting, cooking, stir-frying, etc. Combine these steps in an orderly way to become a dish with colour and flavour.

The Daojing, an ancient book in China, once said that "Governing a big country is as delicate as frying a small fish". Although cooking a dish doesn't seem to have anything to do with Computational Thinking. I'm learning to communicate with something to share such things one day to teach them how to cook. Computational Thinking is a course that teaches us how to communicate with computers, to better express what we want computers to do by learning their way of reflection, and to ensure that computers can execute our instructions according to our wishes. Generally speaking, Computational Thinking consists of four main parts: disassembly, encapsulation, pattern recognition and Algorithm. Each component complements the other and becomes a unique mode of thought, which enables us to model real problems as a workflow that conforms to the working principle of the computer. And guide us in the follow-up operation to transform these processes into executable computer programs.

Computational Thinking includes Decomposition, Abstraction, Pattern Recognition, and Algorithm design. Just as we try to divide complex mathematical formulas into simple forms every time, we can break down significant problems into smaller ones through decomposition. We can solve them with some simple operations, principles and conditions. Abstraction helps us to combine small modules into a complete system, enabling us to reuse some parts independently while focusing more on a specific small-scale system at the same time rather than always taking into account the rest of the whole system. Pattern Recognition is by observing the laws of things and abstracting them, just like finding the formula applicable to the problem and using the formula to solve the answer.

And Algorithm is to construct a set of steps that can solve problems, and we can follow this series of steps to drive the computer so that it can solve real problems. Computational Thinking provides a set of thought tools that enable us to understand the logic of the computer, thus better control the computer, understand how the computer behaves under one control, and design better control signals.

Although Computational Thinking will still make outstanding achievements in subsequent computer science-related courses, it has impacted my life, especially my habit of using computers.

For example, I began to pay attention to the accuracy of an operation or feedback. My computer and mobile phone can accurately give feedback on the information I want, especially being able to accurately adjust some parameters. With the resolution of photos produced by my mobile camera, I began to pay attention to the actual resolution of the photo quality rather than rough descriptors such as "extremely high" or "standard".

Another change is that after accumulating some knowledge, I began to pay attention to the encapsulation and reuse of the various tools. I began studying the actual paths and commands behind some shortcuts on Windows computers and created shortcuts according to my needs. For example, when I need to start a virtual environment for Python, I can make a shortcut that can quickly call cmd.exe, cd to the folder I need, and run venv's activate script. This dramatically facilitates my efficiency every time I write Python code.

Computational Thinking has brought me much more than that, but I have started here and will continue to work hard to keep improving.
